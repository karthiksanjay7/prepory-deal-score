Prepory Deal Score

How we score every deal on fit and engagement to prioritize the hottest leads

The Prepory Deal Score is a lead scoring system built inside HubSpot that automatically ranks every deal in our pipeline. It works by evaluating two things: fit (how well the lead matches our ideal customer based on their lead source, product interest, and buying signals) and engagement (how actively they interact with us through email opens, link clicks, and website visits). These two scores combine into a single number on each deal record, along with a tier label from A1 (hottest) to C3 (coldest). The score updates in real time as properties change and contacts take actions, giving enrollment officers a clear, data-driven way to know which deals to prioritize first. No manual input is needed — the system scores every deal automatically.

How We Built the Formula

The scoring was designed mathematically so both fit and engagement can realistically reach 100, and the A/B/C thresholds reflect actual deal quality.

The design principle: A deal picks exactly one lead channel and one product tier. Signals and priority are optional bonuses. So the formula is: Channel + Tier + Signals + Priority = 100 max. We weighted each group by how well it predicts deal value, then set thresholds based on where the 35 possible channel-tier combinations naturally cluster.

Group Weights

Each group gets a percentage of the 100-point budget based on its predictive value.

Product Tier — 40%
Channel — 30%
Signals — 20%
Priority — 10%
Why Product Tier gets 40%: The program a deal is interested in directly determines revenue. A Synthesis deal (Tier 5) is worth significantly more than a Spark deal (Tier 1). This is the strongest predictor of deal value.

Why Lead Channel gets 30%: Where the lead came from drives conversion likelihood. Forbes leads have the highest intent; organic and referral leads behave differently. This is the second-best predictor.

Why Signals get 20%: Re-engagement after going quiet (+15) and competitor awareness (+5) are strong intent signals, but they're optional bonuses — not every deal triggers them. They serve as tie-breakers that push borderline deals up a tier.

Why Priority gets 10%: A manual flag from the team. Important as a human override, but shouldn't dominate the data-driven score.

Where the lead came from. Based on the associated contact's Lead Channel property. Points decrease using a decay curve where each step down loses ~15-20% of value.

Engagement Score — Point Values Event-Based Max 100

Points from actions the contact takes. All events use "Score every time" — points stack with each occurrence. Values are deliberately low because they accumulate over many interactions.

Why engagement values are small numbers: Unlike fit (where you pick one channel and one tier), engagement stacks every single time. A contact might open 10+ emails over a deal cycle. Even +2 per open becomes 20+ points quickly. Small per-event values prevent score inflation while still rewarding consistent engagement.

Event Value Hierarchy

Not all interactions are equal. Events are ranked by how strongly they signal buying intent.

Sales open (7) > Sales click (5) > Mktg click (4) > Page visit (3) > Mktg open (2)

Sales Emails (1:1) — strongest signal

Personal emails sent by enrollment officers directly from HubSpot (post-IC follow-ups, profile plans, etc.)

EventPoints (each time)
Sales email open+7
Sales email click+5
Why sales email open is worth the most (7): When a contact opens the enrollment officer's personal email — a post-IC follow-up, a profile plan, a next-steps message — that's the strongest buying signal we track. It means they're actively reading personal communication from the person who will close the deal. This is more important than clicking a link, because the open itself proves they're engaged with the EO relationship.

Why sales click is 5 (not higher): A click inside a sales email is valuable, but the open already captured the key signal. The click adds to it but doesn't double it.

Marketing Emails — medium signal

Bulk campaigns, newsletters, and automated workflow emails.

EventPoints (each time)
Clicked link in email+4
Opened email+2
Why marketing click is 4: Clicking through on a campaign shows genuine interest — they wanted to learn more. But it's a mass email, not personal, so it's worth less than sales email interactions.
Why marketing open is only 2: This is the weakest signal. Email opens can be triggered by auto-preview in some email clients. It still counts (repeated opens show the contact is seeing your brand), but each one contributes minimally.

Page Visits — passive interest

EventPoints (each time)
Page visited+3
Why page visit is 3: Browsing the website shows active research — more intentional than passively opening an email (2), but less decisive than clicking a specific link in a campaign (4). A contact visiting 5 pages earns 15 points, which alone won't push them to A tier but contributes meaningfully alongside email engagement.

Supporting Workflows

These workflows feed data into properties that the scoring reads. The scoring app doesn't run these — they run independently.

WorkflowWhat It DoesPowers
WF1 — Auto-Populate Lead Channel Copies the contact's source into the deal's Lead Channel property Fit → Lead Channel group
WF2 — Re-engagement Tracker Watches for 10+ days inactivity, then sets "Re-engaged = Yes" when contact comes back Fit → Re-engaged (+15)
WF3 — Auto-Populate First Conversation Sets the First Conversation property from the contact's initial response Fit → Competitor Awareness (+5)
Engagement events don't need workflows. HubSpot automatically tracks email opens, clicks, and page visits. It also automatically categorizes emails as "Marketing Email" (bulk/automated) vs "Sales Email 1:1" (sent personally by an EO from the CRM). No setup needed for these — just add the criteria in the Lead Scoring app.
